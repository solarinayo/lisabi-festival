Lisabi — History & Legacy

Historical Background

Between 1770–1780, Lisabi led the revolt against the Oyo Empire, uniting Egba farmers under the Egbe Aaro. His vision was of resilience, freedom, and dignity for his people. After the victory, he disappeared into the Igbo Lisabi Sacred Forest, where his spirit is believed to dwell.

Why It Matters

His enduring legacy lives on in the phrase “Egba, omo Lisabi” — a timeless reminder of unity, self-determination, and cultural pride. The Lisabi Festival is more than celebration; it is a bridge between heritage and modern identity.



Festival Schedule & Highlights

Day 1: Opening Ceremony

Historic gun salute, drumming, and storytelling sessions at Olumo Rock. Reconnect with Abeokuta’s roots while enjoying the iconic view.

Day 2: Palace Visit & Youth Mobilization

Participate in youth-led palace cleaning and community mobilization. Experience Yoruba rites and historical Igbo Lisabi insights.

Day 3: Cultural Exhibitions & Competitions

Games, quiz finals, exhibitions, and seminars exploring Abeokuta’s art, culture, and cuisine.

Day 4: Processions & Musical Performances

Grand processions from Igbo Lisabi to Olumo Rock with drummers, dancers, masquerades, and evening Afrobeat concerts.

Day 5: Closing Gala & Heritage Celebration

Afro-fusion concert, storytelling, awards, crafts, and culinary delights at Central Market. Farewell ceremonies for diaspora and locals alike.
